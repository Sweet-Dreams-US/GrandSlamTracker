# Monster Remodeling - Client Requirements

## Business Overview
- **Industry**: Home Remodeling/Construction
- **Location**: Fort Wayne, IN
- **Status**: Active prospect
- **Baseline**: ~$34,000/mo ($408,000/yr)
- **Target**: 50-100% growth

## Unique Features Required

### 1. Revenue Streams with Material Costs
Each revenue stream tracks material cost percentage because remodeling has significant COGS:
- Kitchen Remodels (40% materials)
- Bathroom Remodels (30% materials)
- Basement Finishing (35% materials)
- Additions (45% materials)

### 2. Business Cost Modeling
Detailed operational cost structure for profitability analysis:
- **Crews**: Members, cost per member per job, avg job revenue
- **Other Employees**: Administrative staff
- **Overhead Costs**: General business expenses
- **Insurance**: Liability, workers comp
- **Vehicles**: Fleet costs
- **Tools & Equipment**: Depreciation, repairs
- **Marketing**: Current spend
- **Office Rent**: If applicable

### 3. Growth Target Options
- **Percentage-based**: 25%, 50%, 75%, 100%, 150%
- **Revenue target**: Input specific annual revenue goal (e.g., $600,000/yr)
- System auto-calculates required growth % from revenue target

### 4. Revenue Override Mode
- Edit specific months manually to model "what-if" scenarios
- Recalculates all fees when overrides applied
- Visual indicator when using custom values vs projected

### 5. Fort Wayne Market Intelligence
Built-in market data for context:
- TAM/SAM calculations
- Housing stock analysis by era
- Competitor landscape by tier
- Growth scenario modeling (boutique vs volume vs design-build)

## Seasonal Factors
Remodeling follows construction seasonality:
- Peak: Spring/Summer (Mar-Aug) - best weather for work
- Dip: Winter (Dec-Feb) - weather delays, holidays
- Shoulder: Fall (Sep-Nov) - finishing season

## Contract Terms
Standard Grand Slam terms apply:
- Year 1: Growth fee only (no foundation/sustaining)
- Seasonal baseline adjustments account for construction patterns
- Tiered growth fee percentages based on baseline tier
