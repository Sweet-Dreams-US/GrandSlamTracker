# Renewal Criteria

## Renewal Decision Framework

Use this document at end of contract term to evaluate whether to renew, renegotiate, or part ways.

---

## Renewal Decision Matrix

Score each factor. Total determines action.

| Metric | Renew (2) | Renegotiate (1) | Don't Renew (0) | Score |
|--------|-----------|-----------------|-----------------|-------|
| Avg Monthly Fee | >$1,500 | $800-1,500 | <$800 | ______ |
| Relationship | Good | Okay | Difficult | ______ |
| Growth Achieved | >50% | 20-50% | <20% | ______ |
| Payment History | On time | Mostly on time | Late/problems | ______ |
| Effort Required | Reasonable | High but okay | Excessive | ______ |
| Future Potential | Growing | Uncertain | Declining | ______ |
| **TOTAL** | | | | ______ |

### Scoring Guide

| Score | Action |
|-------|--------|
| **10-12** | Renew with standard terms |
| **7-9** | Renegotiate terms |
| **4-6** | Consider not renewing |
| **0-3** | Don't renew |

## Renewal Scenarios

### Scenario A: Success Story

**Profile:**
- Growth achieved: 75%+
- Average monthly fees: $2,000+
- Relationship: Strong
- Payment: Always on time
- Matrix Score: 10-12

**Action:** Renew with standard terms

**Conversation Template:**
```
Subject: Excited to Continue Our Partnership

Hi [Name],

What a year! Your business grew [X]% and we're thrilled to have been
part of that journey.

For Year 2, here's what changes:
- New baseline: $[X] (reflecting your growth)
- Your foundation fee adjusts to: $[X]
- Sustaining fee kicks in at: $[X]/month
- Growth fees continue on new uplift

Ready to keep the momentum going? Let's schedule a quick call to
discuss Year 2 strategy.

[Signature]
```

---

### Scenario B: Moderate Success

**Profile:**
- Growth achieved: 30-50%
- Average monthly fees: $1,200-1,800
- Relationship: Good
- Payment: Generally on time
- Matrix Score: 7-9

**Action:** Renew with minor adjustments

**Considerations:**
- Add minimum fee if not already present
- Discuss what held back faster growth
- Set clearer Year 2 expectations

**Contract Modifications:**
- Add $500-750/month minimum
- Tighten scope to priority channels
- Add quarterly review checkpoints

---

### Scenario C: Underperformance

**Profile:**
- Growth achieved: 10-25%
- Average monthly fees: $600-1,000
- Relationship: Okay
- Payment: Mostly on time
- Matrix Score: 4-6

**Action:** Renegotiate or part ways

**Options to Present:**

| Option | Terms | Our Protection |
|--------|-------|---------------|
| Add minimum fee | $750/month floor | Guaranteed income |
| Convert to hybrid | $1,000 retainer + 50% growth fees | Baseline covered |
| Reduce scope | Strategy only, no execution | Less effort |
| Shorten term | 6 months instead of 12 | Earlier exit option |

**Conversation Template:**
```
Subject: Year 2 Discussion

Hi [Name],

Thanks for a year of partnership. I want to be direct with you about
where we stand.

Your business grew [X]%, which is progress—but our fee structure
resulted in [average] monthly fees that are below our sustainability
threshold.

For Year 2 to work, we need to adjust our arrangement. Here are some
options:

1. Add a $750/month minimum fee
2. Convert to a $1,000/month retainer with reduced growth fees
3. Reduce our scope to strategy-only

I'd like to discuss which option works best for you. If none of these
fit, I understand—we can part ways professionally.

[Signature]
```

**Profile:**

- Average monthly fees: <$1,000 (due to low baseline)

- Payment: On time

## Renegotiation Options Menu

### Option 1: Add Minimum Fee

| Current Average | Minimum to Add |
|-----------------|---------------|
| $500-800 | $500/month |
| $800-1,200 | $750/month |

**How it works:**
- Client pays higher of: calculated fees OR minimum
- Foundation fee remains the same
- Growth fees calculate normally
- Minimum just sets a floor

---

### Option 2: Convert to Hybrid

| Component | Terms |
|-----------|-------|
| Retainer | $750-1,500/month (based on scope) |
| Foundation Fee | Waived |
| Growth Fees | 50% of standard rates |

**When to use:**
- Client wants budget predictability
- We need guaranteed baseline
- Growth is uncertain

---

### Option 3: Reduce Scope

| Full Service | Strategy Only |
|--------------|---------------|
| Full execution on all platforms | Strategy + reporting only |
| Content creation | Templates and guidelines |
| Daily management | Monthly reviews |
| Full fee structure | 50% of standard fees |

**When to use:**
- Client has internal team
- Our effort is too high for fees
- They want to DIY with guidance

---

### Option 4: Shorten Term

| Standard | Short Term |
|----------|------------|
| 12 months | 6 months |
| Annual reviews | Quarterly reviews |
| Standard notice | 30-day exit clause |

**When to use:**
- Uncertainty about fit
- Testing renegotiated terms
- Either party wants flexibility

---

### Option 5: Performance Milestone Exit

Add clause: *"If monthly fees average below $[X] for any rolling 3-month period, either party may terminate with 30 days notice."*

**When to use:**
- Renegotiating with uncertain client
- Protecting against prolonged underperformance
- Giving client flexibility they're requesting

## Sustaining Fee Mechanics

### What It Does
Ensures we never earn less than last year's average after baseline reset.

### Formula
```
Sustaining Fee = Last Year Avg Monthly Fee - (New Foundation ÷ 12)
```

### Rules
1. Only kicks in Year 2+
2. Can only increase, never decrease
3. Recalculated at each annual reset

### Worked Example: Monster Remodeling

**Year 1 Performance:**
- Baseline: $380,000/year
- Actual Revenue: $725,000
- Average Monthly Fee: $3,200

**Year 2 Setup:**
- New Baseline: $380,000 × 1.12 (industry) × 1.35 (retention) = $574,560
- New Foundation: $574,560 × 2.0% = $11,491/year
- Monthly Foundation: $11,491 ÷ 12 = $958/month

**Sustaining Fee Calculation:**
```
Sustaining = $3,200 - $958 = $2,242/month
```

**Result:** In Year 2, we start every month with $958 (foundation) + $2,242 (sustaining) = $3,200 before any growth fees.

## Baseline Reset Mechanics

### Formula
```
New Baseline = Old Baseline × Industry Factor × (1 + Growth% × Retention%)
```

### Industry Growth Factors

| Industry | Factor | Notes |
|----------|--------|-------|
| Remodeling | 1.12 | Strong demand |
| Home Services | 1.10 | Essential |
| Healthcare | 1.08 | Aging population |
| Legal | 1.06 | Stable |
| Real Estate | 1.08 | Market dependent |
| Retail | 1.04 | High competition |
| Restaurants | 1.05 | Thin margins |
| Entertainment | 1.10 | Discretionary |
| Professional Services | 1.06 | Stable |
| Automotive | 1.05 | Steady |
| Beauty | 1.04 | Competitive |
| Fitness | 1.08 | Growing |

**Default:** 1.06 if industry not listed or uncertain

### Retention Options

| Option | Retention % | Best For |
|--------|-------------|----------|
| Conservative | 25% | Keep more growth opportunity |
| Moderate | 35% | Default for most clients |
| Aggressive | 50% | Client negotiated, high retention expected |

### Worked Example

**Client:** Monster Remodeling
- Old Baseline: $380,000/year
- Industry: Remodeling (1.12 factor)
- Growth Achieved: 90%
- Retention Option: Moderate (35%)

**Calculation:**
```
New Baseline = $380,000 × 1.12 × (1 + 0.90 × 0.35)
New Baseline = $380,000 × 1.12 × 1.315
New Baseline = $559,664
```

**Rounded:** $560,000/year new baseline
