# Document 21: Annual 1099-NEC Filing Checklist

**Frequency:** Annual (prepared in January, filed by January 31)

## 1. Purpose

To provide a comprehensive, step-by-step checklist for filing **IRS Form 1099-NEC** (Non-Employee Compensation) for every contractor who received payments at or above the reporting threshold during the calendar year. This document is the final step in the contractor payment lifecycle — it transforms the year-round tracking in Documents 06 and 16 into filed tax forms.

---

## 2. Key Deadlines — 2026 Payments (Filed in Early 2027)

| Action | Deadline | Extension Available? |
|---|---|---|
| **Furnish Copy B to contractors** | **January 31, 2027** | **NO** — 1099-NEC has no extension |
| **File Copy A with IRS** | **January 31, 2027** | **NO** — same firm deadline |
| **E-filing required** | If filing **10+ total** information returns (any type) | N/A |
| **Corrected 1099-NEC** | As soon as error is discovered | N/A |

> **Critical:** There is NO extension for 1099-NEC filing. Unlike 1099-MISC (which can be extended to March 31 for e-filing), the 1099-NEC deadline is firm. Plan accordingly.

## 3. Reporting Threshold

### For Payments Made in Calendar Year 2026

| Threshold | Amount | Authority |
|---|---|---|
| **1099-NEC Required** | **$2,000 or more** in total payments to a single contractor | One Big Beautiful Bill Act (2025) — effective for 2026 payments |
| **Prior threshold (2025 and earlier)** | $600 or more | IRC § 6041(a) |

> **Best Practice:** Even for contractors below $2,000, retain all payment records. Thresholds may change, and the contractor is still required to report the income on their own tax return regardless of whether they receive a 1099.

## 4. 1099-NEC Preparation Checklist

### Phase 1: Pre-Preparation (December 1–31)

- [ ] Pull the **1099 Contractor YTD Tracker** (Document 16) — review all contractor totals
- [ ] Identify every contractor with YTD payments **≥ $2,000**
- [ ] Verify **W-9 on file** for every contractor requiring a 1099
- [ ] If W-9 is missing or outdated, contact contractor **immediately** to request updated form
- [ ] Confirm legal names, addresses, and TINs (SSN/EIN) match W-9s
- [ ] Reconcile YTD totals against bank statements / payment records

### Phase 2: Form Preparation (January 1–15)

For each contractor requiring a 1099-NEC, complete:

| 1099-NEC Box | Description | Source |
|---|---|---|
| **Payer information** | Sweet Dreams Media LLC name, address, EIN | Business records |
| **Recipient information** | Contractor legal name, address, TIN (from W-9) | W-9 on file |
| **Box 1** | Nonemployee compensation — total calendar year payments | Document 16 YTD total |
| **Box 4** | Federal income tax withheld | $0 (unless backup withholding was applied) |
| **Box 5** | State tax withheld | $0 (Indiana does not require IC withholding) |
| **Box 6** | State/Payer's state number | Indiana TID |
| **Box 7** | State income | Same as Box 1 |

### Phase 3: Review (January 15–20)

- [ ] Both partners (Jay and Cole) review all prepared 1099-NECs
- [ ] Verify Box 1 amounts match Document 16 YTD totals exactly
- [ ] Verify recipient information matches W-9s exactly
- [ ] Check for any missed contractors
- [ ] Resolve any discrepancies before mailing/filing

### Phase 4: Distribution & Filing (January 20–31)

- [ ] **Print/prepare Copy B** for each contractor (recipient's copy)
- [ ] **Mail Copy B** to each contractor by January 31 (or deliver electronically with consent)
- [ ] **File Copy A with IRS:**
  - **E-file** (required if 10+ total information returns): Use IRS FIRE system, approved e-file provider, or tax software
  - **Paper file** (if fewer than 10 returns): Mail Form 1096 (transmittal) + 1099-NEC Copy A to IRS
- [ ] **Indiana filing:** If using IRS Combined Federal/State Filing (CF/SF) program, Indiana automatically receives copies. If paper filing, submit copies to Indiana DOR separately.
- [ ] Save all filing confirmations

## 6. Rules & Laws

### 6.1 IRC § 6041 — Information Returns

Requires payers to file information returns for payments of $600+ (general rule) to persons engaged in a trade or business. The One Big Beautiful Bill Act increased this to $2,000 for 1099-NEC specifically, effective for 2026 payments.

### 6.2 IRC § 6071(c) — 1099-NEC Due Date

Sets the firm January 31 deadline for 1099-NEC. No extension is available regardless of filing method.

### 6.3 IRC § 3406 — Backup Withholding

If a contractor:
- Refuses to provide a W-9, OR
- Provides an incorrect TIN, OR
- The IRS notifies Sweet Dreams of underreporting

Then Sweet Dreams must withhold **24% of each payment** and remit it to the IRS using **Form 945** (Annual Return of Withheld Federal Income Tax). This is reported in **Box 4** of the 1099-NEC.

### 6.4 Penalties for Late or Incorrect Filing

| Violation | Penalty Per Form | Annual Cap (Small Business ≤ $5M) |
|---|---|---|
| 1–30 days late | $60 | $220,500 |
| 31+ days but by August 1 | $130 | $551,500 |
| After August 1 or not filed | $340 | $1,102,500 |
| Intentional disregard | $680 | **No cap** |

### 6.5 Corrected 1099-NEC

If an error is discovered after filing:

- **Type 1 Correction** (wrong amount): File a corrected 1099-NEC checking the "CORRECTED" box with the right amount. Send updated Copy B to contractor.
- **Type 2 Correction** (wrong recipient info): File two corrected forms — one to zero out the wrong entry, one with the correct information.

## 7. Filing Methods

### E-Filing (Required for 10+ Returns)

| Method | Details |
|---|---|
| **IRS FIRE System** | Free, requires registration at fire.irs.gov |
| **Tax Software** | TurboTax Business, TaxAct, FreeTaxUSA (1099 filing add-on) |
| **Third-Party E-File** | Tax1099.com, Track1099.com, eFile4Biz.com (fees apply) |

### Paper Filing (< 10 Returns)

| Item | Details |
|---|---|
| **Form 1096** | Transmittal form that accompanies paper 1099-NECs to the IRS |
| **1099-NEC forms** | Must use official IRS forms (red ink Copy A) — NOT printable from IRS.gov |
| **Order forms** | Order from IRS.gov or purchase from office supply stores |
| **Mail to** | Department of the Treasury, Internal Revenue Service Center, Austin, TX 73301 (or per 1096 instructions) |

## 9. Common Mistakes to Avoid

| Mistake | Consequence | Prevention |
|---|---|---|
| Using contractor's nickname instead of legal name | IRS mismatch → penalty notice | Always use name from W-9 |
| Wrong SSN/EIN | IRS TIN mismatch → B-notice | Verify TIN against W-9 before filing |
| Including sales tax in payment totals | Overstated income reported | Only report net payments for services |
| Filing 1099 for payments to corporations | Not required (except attorneys) | Check Box 3 on W-9 (entity type) |
| Missing the January 31 deadline | $60–$340/form penalty | Start prep in December, not January |
| Forgetting Indiana state filing | State compliance issue | Use CF/SF program for automatic state filing |

## 10. Document Retention

- **Retain filed 1099-NECs and 1096 for 7 years** from filing date.
- **Retain W-9 forms for 4 years** after the last year they were used.
- **Retain all supporting documents (Document 06, Document 16) for 7 years.**
