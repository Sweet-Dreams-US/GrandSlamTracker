## 1. Purpose

To organize all data and documentation required to prepare and file **IRS Form 1065** (U.S. Return of Partnership Income) for Sweet Dreams Media LLC. This is the partnership's **annual federal informational tax return** — it does not result in the partnership paying tax, but it reports all income, deductions, and partner allocations to the IRS.

The 1065 generates the **Schedule K-1s** that Jay and Cole need to file their individual returns.

## 2. Filing Deadlines

| Item | Deadline |

| **Late filing penalty** | **$250/month per partner** for up to 12 months = max **$6,000** for 2-partner LLC |

## 6. Rules & Laws

### 6.1 Partnership Filing Status

Sweet Dreams Media LLC, as a multi-member LLC that has NOT elected S-corp status, is treated as a **partnership** for federal tax purposes by default under **Treasury Regulation § 301.7701-3**.

The partnership files Form 1065 but does **not pay federal income tax**. All income, deductions, gains, losses, and credits pass through to Jay and Cole on their individual returns via Schedule K-1.

### 6.2 IRC § 6698 — Late Filing Penalty

If Form 1065 is filed late (without an extension), the penalty is:

- **$250 per partner per month** (or partial month)
- Up to **12 months**
- For Sweet Dreams (2 partners): max penalty = **$6,000**

> **First-Time Penalty Abatement:** If this is the first time Sweet Dreams has filed late and has a clean compliance history, the IRS may waive the penalty upon request.

### 6.3 Tax Basis Reporting

Starting with tax year 2023, partnerships are required to report **partner tax basis capital accounts** on Schedule K-1 using the **tax basis method**. This means:

- Track each partner's initial capital contributions
- Add: income allocated, additional contributions
- Subtract: losses allocated, distributions, guaranteed payments drawn beyond allocated income
- Report the resulting balance on Schedule K-1, Item L

### 6.4 Qualified Business Income (QBI) — Section 199A

The partnership must report **QBI information** on each Schedule K-1 to enable partners to calculate their §199A deduction:

- QBI = Ordinary business income (Box 1) — does NOT include guaranteed payments
- Report on K-1, Box 20, Code Z
- Media production is generally NOT a Specified Service Trade or Business (SSTB), so the deduction should be available regardless of income level
