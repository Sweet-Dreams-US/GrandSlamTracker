## 1. Purpose

To maintain a **real-time year-to-date view** of every payment made to every independent contractor, organized for 1099-NEC filing preparation. This document answers three critical questions at any point during the year:

1. **Who have we paid, and how much?**
2. **Who is approaching or has crossed the 1099 reporting threshold?**
3. **Do we have all required documentation (W-9, IC agreement) on file?**

This tracker prevents the December scramble of trying to reconstruct a year's worth of contractor payments from bank statements.

## 5. Rules & Laws

### 5.1 Form 1099-NEC Filing Requirements (2026 Payments)

| Requirement | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Threshold** | $2,000 in aggregate payments during calendar year 2026 |
| **Form** | 1099-NEC (Non-Employee Compensation) |
| **Copy B to Contractor** | Due by **January 31, 2027** |
| **Copy A to IRS** | Due by **January 31, 2027** |
| **E-Filing Required** | If filing **10 or more** information returns in total (any type) |
| **Extensions** | **NOT AVAILABLE** for 1099-NEC |

### 5.2 What Payments Are Reportable on 1099-NEC

| Payment Type | Reportable? | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Engineer session payouts | **Yes** | Services performed for the business |
| Producer beat sale payouts | **Yes** | Commissions/fees for services |
| Freelancer project payments | **Yes** | Services performed for the business |
| Reimbursement of expenses | **Depends** | Accountable plan reimbursements = No; non-accountable = Yes |
| Payments to corporations | **Generally No** | Except for legal fees (attorneys) and medical/health payments |

### 5.3 Consequences of Missing or Incorrect 1099s

| Issue | Consequence |
|---|---|
| Fail to issue 1099-NEC | Penalty $60–$340/return + potential loss of deduction |
| Incorrect information on 1099 | Same penalties as failure to file |
| Fail to collect W-9 | Must impose 24% backup withholding on all payments |
| Contractor disputes amount | Use this tracker + Document 06 as evidence |

### 5.4 Indiana 1099 Filing

Indiana requires 1099 filing through the **Combined Federal/State Filing (CF/SF) program**. If you e-file 1099-NECs with the IRS, Indiana automatically receives a copy. If you paper-file, you must also submit copies to the Indiana DOR.

## 6. Business Relation

### 6.1 Why Track This Throughout the Year

Waiting until December to figure out who needs a 1099 creates:

- **Missing W-9s** — Contractor moved, changed number, or is unresponsive
- **Incorrect amounts** — Bank records don't clearly show which payments were to which contractor
- **Missed deadlines** — January 31 comes fast and there are no extensions

By updating this tracker bi-weekly (when payouts are made), year-end filing becomes a **10-minute verification** instead of a **10-hour scramble**.

### 6.2 The W-9 Checkpoint

This tracker serves as the **compliance checkpoint** for W-9 collection:

| Status | Action |
|---|---|
| ✅ W-9 on file, current | No action needed |
| ❌ W-9 missing, no payments yet | Collect before first payment |
| ❌ W-9 missing, payments already made | **Urgent:** Contact contractor immediately. If unresponsive, begin backup withholding (24%) on future payments |
| ⚠️ W-9 on file, but address/TIN may have changed | Request updated W-9 before year-end |

## 8. Document Retention

- Retain this tracker and all supporting payment records for **7 years** from the date the 1099-NECs were filed.
- Retain W-9 forms for **4 years** after the last tax year they were used.
- This is the definitive document if the IRS questions 1099 filings.
