## 6. Rules & Laws

### 6.1 Indiana Estimated Tax Requirement

Under **IC § 6-3-4-4.1**, individuals must make estimated tax payments if they expect to owe **$1,000 or more** in Indiana income tax for the year after credits and withholding.

**Safe Harbor (avoid penalty):**

- Pay at least **100% of the prior year's Indiana tax liability** in equal quarterly installments, OR
- Pay at least **90% of the current year's Indiana tax liability**

### 6.2 Indiana Underpayment Penalty

Under **IC § 6-3-4-4.2**, the penalty for underpayment of estimated tax is calculated at the **DOR-published interest rate** (adjusted annually) on the underpaid amount for the period of underpayment.

**Exception:** No penalty if total tax liability for the year is under $1,000.

### 6.3 Indiana Partnership Pass-Through Entity Tax (PTE) Election

Indiana offers an **optional pass-through entity tax (PTE)** under IC § 6-3-2-1.5. If elected:

- The **partnership itself** pays Indiana income tax on behalf of the partners.
- Partners receive a **dollar-for-dollar state tax credit** on their individual returns.
- The payment is deductible on the **federal partnership return** (Form 1065), effectively circumventing the $10,000 SALT deduction cap.

> **Recommendation for Sweet Dreams:** If Jay and Cole itemize deductions on their federal returns (rather than taking the standard deduction), the PTE election could save federal taxes by converting a non-deductible state tax payment into a deductible partnership expense. Discuss with your CPA — the benefit depends on total income levels and other deductions.

### 6.4 Indiana Individual Filing

Each partner files **Indiana Form IT-40** annually. Partnership income is reported on **Schedule IN K-1** which accompanies the individual return. The annual return is due **April 15** of the following year (or the 15th day of the 4th month after year-end).
