## 1. Purpose

To produce a complete Profit & Loss (Income) Statement for Sweet Dreams Media LLC each month. This is the **financial health check** that shows whether the business made or lost money, where the money came from, and where it went. It combines revenue data (Document 08) with expense data (Document 04) to produce the bottom line.

This P&L maps directly to the line items on **IRS Form 1065** (U.S. Return of Partnership Income), making annual tax preparation straightforward.

## 4. Rules & Laws

### 4.1 Partnership Tax Return — Form 1065

Sweet Dreams LLC must file **IRS Form 1065** annually (due **March 15** of the following year, with a 6-month extension available via Form 7004). The monthly P&L, accumulated over 12 months, provides all the data needed to complete Form 1065.

**Key Points:**

- The partnership itself does **not pay income tax**. It files an informational return.
- All income and deductions "pass through" to Jay and Cole on their individual **Schedule K-1s**.
- The partnership may owe a penalty if it fails to file Form 1065 on time: **$250/month per partner** (2026 rate — verify annually).

### 4.2 Self-Employment Tax Implications

Both Jay and Cole owe **self-employment tax** on:

- Their Guaranteed Payments (Box 4a of K-1)
- Their share of Ordinary Business Income (Box 1 of K-1)

**2026 SE Tax Rates:**

| Component | Rate | Wage Base |
|---|---|---|
| Social Security | 12.4% | First $168,600 of net SE earnings (verify for 2026) |
| Medicare | 2.9% | All net SE earnings |
| Additional Medicare | 0.9% | Over $200,000 (single) / $250,000 (married filing jointly) |

> The SE tax is calculated on **92.35%** of net self-employment income (the 7.65% deduction simulates the "employer half" that W-2 employees get automatically).

### 4.3 Qualified Business Income Deduction (Section 199A)

Partners in a pass-through entity may be eligible for a **20% deduction** on qualified business income (QBI). However:

- Guaranteed Payments are **excluded from QBI**. Only the partner's share of ordinary business income (Box 1 of K-1) qualifies.
- The deduction is subject to income phase-outs and specified service trade or business (SSTB) limitations.
- Media/video production is generally NOT classified as an SSTB, so Sweet Dreams partners should qualify if within income thresholds.

> **Strategy:** This is why it's important to balance guaranteed payments vs. profit distributions. Excessive guaranteed payments reduce the QBI-eligible income. Consult your CPA on the optimal split.

## 5. Business Relation

### 5.1 The Monthly P&L Tells the Story

This is the document Jay and Cole should review together every month. It answers:

- Are we growing month-over-month?
- Which revenue stream is strongest?
- Are our expenses under control relative to the 35% business fund?
- How much is flowing to each partner?
- What will our estimated tax payments be?

### 5.2 Breakeven Analysis

```
Monthly Fixed Costs (approximate):
  Rent:           $___
  Insurance:      $___
  Software:       $___
  Utilities:      $___
  Other fixed:    $___
  TOTAL FIXED:    $___ /month

Breakeven Revenue = TOTAL FIXED ÷ 0.35
  (Since 35% of revenue covers overhead)

Example: If fixed costs = $2,000/month
  Breakeven = $2,000 ÷ 0.35 = $5,714/month in gross revenue
```
