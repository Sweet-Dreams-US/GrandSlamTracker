## 1. Purpose

To prepare all data required for filing **Indiana Form IT-65** (Partnership Return) with the Indiana Department of Revenue (DOR). This is the state-level equivalent of the federal Form 1065. Indiana requires every partnership doing business in Indiana or having Indiana-resident partners to file this return.

The IT-65 generates **Indiana Schedule IN K-1** for each partner, which flows to their individual Indiana Form IT-40.

## 4. Rules & Laws

### 4.1 Indiana Code § 6-3-4-12 — Partnership Return Requirement

Every partnership that has gross income derived from Indiana sources, or has a partner that is an Indiana resident, must file Form IT-65. Sweet Dreams qualifies on both counts.

### 4.2 Indiana Adjusted Gross Income Tax

- Indiana's flat income tax rate for individuals: **3.05%** (2026 — verify annually, as it has been declining per IC § 6-3-2-1)
- This applies to each partner's share of partnership income on their individual IT-40
- The partnership itself does NOT pay Indiana income tax (unless the PTE election is made)

### 4.3 County Income Tax

Each partner owes county income tax based on their **county of residence on January 1** of the tax year:

| County | Approximate Rate | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Marion (Indianapolis) | ~2.02% | Most common for Indianapolis-area |
| Hamilton | ~1.00% | Noblesville, Fishers, Carmel |
| Hendricks | ~1.50% | Plainfield, Brownsburg |
| Johnson | ~1.15% | Greenwood, Franklin |
| (Other) | Check DOR website | Rates change annually |

> **Full county rate list:** Available at in.gov/dor → Tax Forms → County Tax Information

### 4.4 Pass-Through Entity Tax (PTE) Election — IC § 6-3-2-1.5

Indiana allows partnerships to **elect** to pay state income tax at the entity level:

| Aspect | Without PTE Election | With PTE Election |
|---|---|---|
| **Who pays IN tax** | Each partner individually | Partnership pays on behalf of partners |
| **State tax rate** | 3.05% (individual rate) | 3.05% (same rate) |
| **Federal deductibility** | Limited by $10,000 SALT cap | Fully deductible as partnership expense |
| **Partner credit** | N/A | Dollar-for-dollar credit on partner's IT-40 |

**When to elect PTE:**
- Partners **itemize** federal deductions (not standard deduction)
- Partners are affected by the **$10,000 SALT cap**
- Total Indiana tax exceeds a few thousand dollars

> **Mechanics:** If elected, the partnership computes tax on its Indiana income and pays it with the IT-65. Each partner then claims a credit for their share on their IT-40, resulting in a net-zero additional liability for the partner but a federal deduction for the partnership.

### 4.5 Composite Return Option — IC § 6-3-4-12(d)

If Sweet Dreams had nonresident partners, it could file a **composite return** on their behalf. Not currently applicable (both partners are Indiana residents), but relevant if the partnership ever adds out-of-state members.

## 5. Indiana Modifications

Indiana starts with federal adjusted gross income and makes certain adjustments:

### Common Additions (increase Indiana income)

| Item | Description | Applies to Sweet Dreams? |
|---|---|---|
| State/local taxes deducted federally | Indiana adds back any state income tax deduction | Only if itemizing (check) |
| Bonus depreciation difference | Indiana may require different depreciation schedule | Check if IN conforms to federal Section 179 |

### Common Subtractions (decrease Indiana income)

| Item | Description | Applies to Sweet Dreams? |
|---|---|---|
| U.S. government interest income | Interest from U.S. savings bonds, etc. | Only if applicable |
| Indiana NOL deduction | Net operating loss carryforward | Only if prior-year losses |

> **Note:** Indiana **conforms** to most federal depreciation rules, including Section 179. Verify annually, as conformity can change.

## 6. IT-65 Preparation Checklist

### Phase 1: Pre-Preparation

- [ ] Federal Form 1065 completed (or near-final draft)
- [ ] Indiana TID number confirmed
- [ ] Partners' county of residence on January 1 verified
- [ ] PTE election decision made (discuss with CPA)
- [ ] Prior year IT-65 available for reference

### Phase 2: Return Preparation

- [ ] Transfer federal income amounts to IT-65
- [ ] Apply Indiana modifications (additions/subtractions)
- [ ] Calculate Indiana distributable income
- [ ] Prepare Schedule IN K-1 for Jay
- [ ] Prepare Schedule IN K-1 for Cole
- [ ] If PTE elected: calculate entity-level tax and payment

### Phase 3: Filing

- [ ] Review all figures for accuracy
- [ ] Both partners review and approve
- [ ] E-file through INTIME or tax software
- [ ] Deliver IN K-1 copies to Jay and Cole
- [ ] Save filing confirmation
