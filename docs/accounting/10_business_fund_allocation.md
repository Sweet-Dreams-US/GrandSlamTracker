## 4. Rules & Laws

### 4.1 Business Expense Deductibility — IRS Publication 535

Every expense paid from the Business Fund must qualify as **"ordinary and necessary"** under IRC § 162(a):

- **Ordinary:** Common and accepted in the media production industry.
- **Necessary:** Helpful and appropriate (does not need to be essential).

If any expense fails this test, it is a **personal expense** and must be reclassified. Personal expenses paid from the business account are treated as **distributions to partners** and are NOT deductible — meaning they increase taxable income without providing a tax benefit.

### 4.2 Mixed-Use Expenses

Some expenses may have both business and personal components:

| Expense | Business Portion | Personal Portion | How to Handle |
|---|---|---|---|
| Cell phone | % used for business calls/texts | % personal use | Deduct business % only |
| Vehicle | % of miles for business travel | % personal | Use mileage log |
| Internet (home office) | % used for business | % personal | Deduct business % only |
| Meals | 100% business purpose | 50% deductible (IRS rule) | Log attendees and purpose |

> **Golden Rule:** When in doubt, err on the side of NOT deducting. An overstated deduction triggers audits; an understated one just means you paid slightly more tax.

### 4.3 Capital Expenditures vs. Current Expenses

| Type | Rule | IRS Treatment |
|---|---|---|
| **Current Expense** (< $2,500 per item) | De minimis safe harbor (Reg. §1.263(a)-1(f)) | Fully deductible in year purchased |
| **Capital Expenditure** (≥ $2,500 per item) | Must capitalize and depreciate | Section 179 or MACRS depreciation |
| **Repair** (restores existing equipment) | Generally current expense | Fully deductible in year paid |
| **Improvement** (betterment, adaptation) | Must capitalize | Section 179 or MACRS depreciation |

> **Sweet Dreams Policy:** Use the **de minimis safe harbor election** to expense all items under $2,500. This election must be made annually by attaching a statement to the tax return. For items ≥ $2,500, use **Section 179** for immediate deduction (recommended during growth phase).

### 4.4 Indiana Business Personal Property Tax

Every tangible business asset — cameras, mics, computers, monitors, audio interfaces, lighting — must be reported on **Indiana Form 103** by **May 15** each year. The assessed value generates a property tax bill from the county.

Track total asset acquisition cost in this document to streamline Form 103 prep (Document 20).

## 5. Business Relation

### 5.1 The 35% Accountability Story

This document tells Jay and Cole whether the 35% is working:

```
Month 1:  Revenue $8,000  | Fund $2,800  | Expenses $2,100  | Surplus $700  ✅
Month 2:  Revenue $6,000  | Fund $2,100  | Expenses $2,200  | Deficit ($100) ⚠️
Month 3:  Revenue $10,000 | Fund $3,500  | Expenses $2,000  | Surplus $1,500 ✅

Cumulative Reserve: $700 + (−$100) + $1,500 = $2,100
```

### 5.2 When to Adjust the 35%

| Signal | Action |
|---|---|
| Consistent monthly surplus > 20% | Consider lowering to 30% (more $ to labor pool) |
| Consistent deficit | Raise to 40% or cut expenses |
| Major equipment purchase needed | Keep at 35% but plan for Section 179 deduction timing |
| Revenue doubles | 35% yields proportionally more — review expense efficiency |

### 5.3 The Surplus Becomes Taxable Profit

Any surplus in the Business Fund at year-end that is NOT spent on deductible business expenses becomes **ordinary business income** on Form 1065, split 50/50 to Jay and Cole. This is why reinvesting in growth is tax-efficient — spent money reduces taxable income; unspent surplus increases it.
