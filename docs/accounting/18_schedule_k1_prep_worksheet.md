## 1. Purpose

To prepare the individual **Schedule K-1 (Form 1065)** for each partner — Jay and Cole. The K-1 is the document that tells each partner exactly how much income, deduction, and credit to report on their personal tax returns. It is the **bridge** between the partnership's Form 1065 and each partner's Form 1040.

Each partner receives their own K-1. This worksheet ensures both K-1s are accurate, consistent, and reconcile back to the partnership return.

## 4. Self-Employment Earnings Calculation (Box 14a)

This is one of the most important boxes. Each partner's net SE earnings include:

```
Box 14a = Guaranteed Payments (Box 4a) + Ordinary Income Share (Box 1)
```

| Component | Jay | Cole |
|---|---|---|
| Guaranteed Payments (Box 4a) | $_____ | $_____ |
| + Ordinary Business Income (Box 1, 50% share) | $_____ | $_____ |
| = **Net SE Earnings (Box 14a)** | **$_____** | **$_____** |

> **Note:** If the partnership has a net loss, Box 14a could be less than guaranteed payments. But guaranteed payments are ALWAYS included in SE earnings, even if the partnership has a loss overall.

Each partner then uses Box 14a on their personal **Schedule SE (Form 1040)** to calculate self-employment tax:

```
SE Tax Base = Box 14a × 0.9235
Social Security = SE Tax Base × 0.124 (up to $168,600 cap)
Medicare = SE Tax Base × 0.029 (no cap)
Additional Medicare = 0.009 × (amount over $200K/$250K)
Total SE Tax = Social Security + Medicare + Additional Medicare
```

**Key Rules:**

- QBI = **Ordinary business income (Box 1) ONLY** — does NOT include guaranteed payments.
- Each partner can potentially deduct **20% of their QBI share** on their personal return, subject to income limitations.
- Since Sweet Dreams is a media production company (NOT a Specified Service Trade or Business), the deduction should be available.
- If total taxable income is below $191,950 (single) / $383,900 (MFJ) for 2026 (verify), the full 20% deduction applies regardless.

> **Tax Planning Implication:** The split between guaranteed payments and profit distributions matters. More profit (Box 1) = more QBI deduction. More guaranteed payments (Box 4a) = less QBI but more defensible with the IRS for unequal work.
