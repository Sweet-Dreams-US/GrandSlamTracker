## 1. Purpose

To calculate and track the **federal quarterly estimated tax payments** that Jay and Cole must each make as partners of Sweet Dreams Media LLC. Because the LLC is a pass-through entity, no federal income tax is withheld at the partnership level. Each partner is personally responsible for making estimated payments to the IRS using **Form 1040-ES** to avoid underpayment penalties.

This document prepares the calculation worksheet that determines how much each partner should pay each quarter.

## 3. Who Must Pay Estimated Taxes

Both Jay and Cole must make quarterly estimated payments if they expect to owe **$1,000 or more** in federal tax for the year after subtracting withholding and credits.

**Safe Harbor Rules (avoid penalties):**

| Method | Requirement |
|---|---|
| **100% of prior year tax** | Pay at least 100% of your 2025 total tax liability, spread across 4 equal payments |
| **90% of current year tax** | Pay at least 90% of your 2026 expected tax liability |
| **110% rule (high earners)** | If prior year AGI > $150,000, must pay 110% of prior year tax to use the safe harbor |

> **Recommended approach for Sweet Dreams:** Use the **100% of prior year tax** method. It's simpler and guaranteed to avoid penalties regardless of income growth.

## 6. Rules & Laws

### 6.1 IRC § 6654 — Failure to Pay Estimated Tax

If a partner underpays estimated taxes, the IRS charges an **underpayment penalty** calculated as interest on the underpaid amount for the period of underpayment. The rate is the **federal short-term rate + 3%** (adjusted quarterly).

**Exceptions (no penalty):**

- Total tax due for the year is less than $1,000
- Total payments (estimated + withholding) ≥ 90% of current year tax
- Total payments ≥ 100% of prior year tax (110% if prior year AGI > $150,000)

### 6.2 Partnership Doesn't Pay Federal Tax

The LLC itself files Form 1065 as an **informational return only**. The partnership pays no federal income tax. All tax liability passes through to Jay and Cole individually.

However, if the partnership fails to file Form 1065 on time, the IRS imposes a penalty of **$250/month per partner** (2026 rate) for up to 12 months = max $6,000 for a 2-partner LLC.

### 6.3 Payment Methods

| Method | How | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| **IRS Direct Pay** | irs.gov/directpay | Free, immediate, from bank account |
| **EFTPS** | eftps.gov | Free, requires enrollment (7-10 days) |
| **Credit/Debit Card** | irs.gov/payments | Processing fee applies (1.85–1.98% credit, $2.20 debit) |
| **Mail** | 1040-ES voucher + check | Slowest — use certified mail with return receipt |

> **Recommendation:** Enroll in **EFTPS** for both Jay and Cole. It allows scheduling payments in advance and provides instant confirmation.

### 7.2 Why Partners Often Underpay

Common mistakes for LLC partners:

- **Forgetting self-employment tax.** SE tax is ~15.3% ON TOP of income tax. Many first-time partners only estimate income tax.
- **Not annualizing.** If Q1 was slow but Q2 boomed, you need to catch up.
- **Ignoring guaranteed payments.** Even if partnership shows a loss, guaranteed payments are still taxable income.

### 7.3 Setting Aside Tax Money

**Recommended:** Each partner should set aside **25–30% of every guaranteed payment** in a personal savings account earmarked for taxes.

```
Jay receives $2,000 guaranteed payment
  → Immediately transfer $600 (30%) to "Tax Savings" account
  → Remaining $1,400 is available for personal use

Quarterly: Pay estimated tax from the Tax Savings account
```
