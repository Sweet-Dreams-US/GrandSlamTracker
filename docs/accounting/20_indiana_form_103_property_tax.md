## 1. Purpose

To inventory all **tangible business personal property** owned by Sweet Dreams Media LLC and prepare the data for filing **Indiana Form 103** (Business Tangible Personal Property Return). Indiana taxes business equipment, furniture, and other tangible assets based on their assessed value — even though they are also depreciable for federal income tax purposes.

Every camera, microphone, computer, monitor, audio interface, lighting rig, and piece of furniture used in the business must be reported.

## 5. Indiana Depreciation & Assessment

Indiana uses its **own depreciation percentages** (not the federal MACRS tables) to determine assessed value. The assessed value is then multiplied by the **local property tax rate** to determine the tax owed.

### Indiana True Tax Value Percentages (General Equipment Pool)

| Age of Property | True Tax Value % of Cost |
|---|---|
| Year 1 (new) | ~50% |
| Year 2 | ~42% |
| Year 3 | ~35% |
| Year 4 | ~30% |
| Year 5 | ~25% |
| Year 6 | ~20% |
| Year 7+ | ~15% (floor) |

> **Note:** Actual percentages vary by property pool (computer equipment, manufacturing, etc.). Use the tables in the Form 103 instructions or the Indiana Department of Local Government Finance (DLGF) guidelines.

### Assessment Calculation Example

```
Asset: Sony A7S III Camera
Acquisition Cost: $3,498.00
Year Acquired: 2024
Filing Year: 2026 (2 years old)
Indiana TV%: 42%

Assessed Value = $3,498 × 0.42 = $1,469.16

Property Tax Rate (example): 2.50 per $100 of assessed value
Annual Tax = ($1,469.16 ÷ 100) × 2.50 = $36.73
```

## 7. Rules & Laws

### 7.1 Indiana Code § 6-1.1-3 — Assessment of Business Personal Property

All tangible personal property used in a trade or business in Indiana is subject to assessment and taxation. The owner of record on **January 1** (the "assessment date") of each year is responsible for filing.

### 7.2 IC § 6-1.1-3-7.2 — Small Business Exemption

Indiana provides an exemption for businesses whose total acquisition cost of personal property in the county is **less than $80,000**:

- If total acquisition cost of all personal property in the county is under $80,000, you may be eligible for an **exemption** from filing Form 103 and paying personal property tax.
- File Form 103-ERA (Exemption Request) to claim this exemption.
- **Sweet Dreams should check:** If total gear + equipment cost is under $80,000, you may not owe any personal property tax.

### 7.3 IC § 6-1.1-37-7 — Assessment Review

If you disagree with the county's assessment, you can file an appeal with the county **Property Tax Assessment Board of Appeals (PTABOA)** within 45 days of the assessment notice.

### 7.4 Relationship to Federal Depreciation

The federal Section 179 deduction or MACRS depreciation does **NOT affect** Indiana personal property tax. Even if you fully expense a $5,000 camera on your federal return using Section 179, you still report its full acquisition cost on Form 103 and Indiana applies its own depreciation schedule.
